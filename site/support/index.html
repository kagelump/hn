Frequently asked

Where does the content come from?

HN Reader displays content from Hacker News via its official public APIs. It is an independent client and is not affiliated with or endorsed by Y Combinator.

Do I need an account?

No. There is no sign-in. The app is read-only — you cannot post, comment, or vote from it.

How do I block an author?

Tap any author's name on a story or comment, then choose Block this user. Their stories are hidden and their comments are replaced with a “[blocked]” placeholder. You can manage and remove blocks under Settings → Blocked Users.

Content policy

HN Reader is a read-only client — you cannot post, comment, or vote from it. It displays content published on Hacker News, which is a moderated platform governed by the Hacker News guidelines and enforced by its own moderators. On top of that, HN Reader lets you block any author so their content no longer appears for you. If you have a concern about the app, contact us at raycatdev@hinoka.org.
