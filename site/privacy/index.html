Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 24, 2026

HN Reader is a client for reading Hacker News content. We designed it to need as little of your data as possible.

What we collect

Nothing. HN Reader has no accounts and no servers of its own. It does not collect personal information, does not use analytics, and does not track your reading or browsing behavior.

Data stored on your device

Your preferences — theme, font, text size, which stories you have visited, and any authors you have blocked — are stored locally on your device using standard app storage. This data never leaves your device and is removed when you delete the app.

Network requests

To show you content, the app fetches data directly from Hacker News and related public services:

  • hacker-news.firebaseio.com — the official Hacker News API, for story data.
  • hn.algolia.com — the Hacker News Search API by Algolia, for comment threads.
  • news.ycombinator.com — for correct comment ordering.
  • The article's own website — when you open reader mode, the app fetches that page directly to display its text.

These services may log standard request metadata (such as IP address and user agent) under their own privacy policies, exactly as a web browser would when visiting them. HN Reader does not send them any personal information.

Children

Hacker News is a moderated public discussion site. HN Reader is a read-only client and also lets you block individual authors (see Support).
