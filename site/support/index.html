Frequently asked

Where does the content come from?

HN Reader displays content from Hacker News via its official public APIs. It is an independent client and is not affiliated with or endorsed by Y Combinator.

Do I need an account?

No. There is no sign-in. The app is read-only — you cannot post, comment, or vote from it.

How do I block an author?

Tap any author's name on a story or comment, then choose Block this user. Their stories and comments (and any replies under them) are hidden immediately. You can manage and remove blocks under Settings → Blocked Users.

How do I report objectionable content?

Tap the author's name on the story or comment and choose Report. This opens a pre-filled email to us with a link to the content. We review reports and act on them promptly.

Content policy

HN Reader shows user-generated content published on Hacker News, which is not created or moderated by us. We do not tolerate objectionable content or abusive behavior. We provide in-app tools to block authors and report content, and we review all reports sent to raycatdev@hinoka.org and act within 24 hours — for example by filtering an author from the app's default experience. Content hosted on Hacker News itself is governed by the Hacker News guidelines.
